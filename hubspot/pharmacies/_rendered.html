CAM+ · Hospital Pharmacies

Certainty for Every Dose You Dispense

Hospital pharmacies hold life-saving medicines that must stay within strict temperature ranges — yet many still rely on clipboards and occasional checks. CAM+ monitors every fridge, freezer, and store room continuously, so compromised stock never reaches a patient and audits never keep you up at night.

The failure you cannot see

There's No Point Giving Medicines That Don't Work

Medicines are not ordinary products. Chemotherapy agents, biologics, blood products, and vaccines are delicate formulations designed to interact with the body in precise ways — and they are unforgiving about how they are stored.

A fridge drifts outside its validated range overnight. By morning, the temperature has recovered. The vials inside look exactly as they did yesterday. Nothing is visibly wrong.

But the potency may be gone — and the impact lands on a patient's treatment, not a spreadsheet. The cost isn't just financial. It's measured in outcomes.

One Excursion. Four Ways It Costs You.

The direct cost is wasted stock. The hidden costs — time, audit stress, lost opportunities — are often larger, and they land on a team already stretched thin.

Patient Safety

The Dose That Looks Fine but Isn’t

A vial of monoclonal antibody therapy that sat through an overnight drift looks identical to one that didn’t. Its potency may be gone. In oncology, immunology, or infectious disease care, an ineffective dose has consequences no one can see coming.

Financial Waste

A Quarter’s Allocation, Gone Overnight

A single batch of advanced cancer drugs can cost tens of thousands of pounds. One excursion can wipe out an entire quarter’s allocation — in a budget environment where every incident of waste is harder to absorb than the last.

Audit Stress

The Logbook That Doesn’t Hold Up

Auditors no longer accept paper logbooks and occasional checks. They expect continuous monitoring, validated systems, and secure audit trails — with evidence that alerts were raised and acted upon. Gaps in documentation carry real consequences.

Hidden Costs

The Hours That Never Reach a Patient

Backtracking, transcribing logs, chasing missing records, reconstructing incidents for auditors. The hidden costs of manual monitoring often exceed the stock loss itself — paid in skilled pharmacy time that should go to clinical work.

The operational reality

More Pressure. Fewer Resources. Manual Systems Break.

While regulatory expectations rise, pharmacy teams are being asked to do more with less — and clipboard-based monitoring is where the strain shows first.

Fewer staff, larger portfolios

ICBs, workforce shortages, and procurement consolidation mean fewer people are responsible for more stock across more sites.

Manual checks across every ward and store

Clipboards, spreadsheets, and disparate devices don’t scale. Hours each week go to repetitive checks and transcription.

Stretched teams miss things

When staff are stretched, manual systems fail: temperatures get missed, alerts are overlooked, data goes unrecorded.

Each lapse compounds the risk

Every gap heightens exposure for patients, budgets, and compliance — and treatments are only getting more storage-sensitive.

The Standard Has Moved

Regulators no longer accept paper logbooks. They expect continuous monitoring, validated systems, and secure audit trails — with proof that alerts are acted upon, not just raised.

Ambient Store Rooms

Controlled ambient storage where summer heat or a failed AC unit quietly takes medicines outside their licensed range.

Door & Seal Events

A fridge door left ajar is the most common cause of excursions. Caught in minutes with an alert, not discovered at the next check.

Humidity

Equipment Health

Power & Environment

Power interruptions and environmental events captured with a complete timeline, ready for the incident review and the auditor.
